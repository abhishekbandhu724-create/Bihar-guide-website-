How to Explore Bihar on a Budget

Bihar is one of India's most affordable states. International travellers can explore everything for ₹1,500–₹2,500 per day including accommodation and food.


All Travel Tips →
✈️

Fly into Gaya or Patna

Gaya Airport has international Buddhist Circuit flights from Bangkok, Colombo & Rangoon. Patna connects to all major Indian cities.

Gaya → Bodh Gaya: 12 km taxi ≈ ₹200
🚂

Travel by Train

Bihar's rail network is excellent and very affordable. Sleeper class connects all major destinations. Book on IRCTC 2–3 weeks ahead.

Patna → Bodh Gaya ≈ ₹80–₹150
🍽️

Eat at Local Dhabas

Litti Chokha, Sattu Paratha, Thekua — authentic Bihari street food is delicious and costs a fraction of restaurant prices.

Full dhaba meal ≈ ₹40–₹80
🛕

Monastery Stays in Bodh Gaya

Thai, Japanese, Tibetan and Bhutanese monasteries offer very affordable rooms to pilgrims and travellers.

₹100–₹300 per night at dharamshalas

Essential Bihar Travel Tips

Everything international tourists need to know before visiting Bihar.

01

Best Time to Visit

October–March is ideal. Cool, dry weather perfect for sightseeing. Avoid May–July (40°C+ heat). Chhath Puja in Oct/Nov is an unmissable spectacle.

02

India e-Visa

Most nationalities can get an India e-Visa at indianvisaonline.gov.in. Apply 4+ days before travel. Cost approx. $25–$80 USD depending on nationality.

03

Entry Point — Gaya or Patna

Gaya Airport has direct Buddhist Circuit flights from Bangkok, Colombo & Rangoon. Patna has flights from Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata.

04

Get a Local SIM

Buy Airtel or Jio SIM at the airport with your passport. ₹299 gives 1.5GB/day for 28 days — essential for Google Maps and booking trains on IRCTC.

05

Currency & Cash

Indian Rupee (INR). ATMs in cities. Carry cash for rural areas — dhabas and village markets are cash-only. $1 USD ≈ ₹83. Cards accepted at hotels.

06

Buddhist Circuit Tour

Bihar Tourism offers guided packages covering Bodh Gaya, Nalanda, Rajgir, Vaishali and Kesaria — perfect for first-time international visitors.

07

Dress Modestly

Cover shoulders and knees at temples and monasteries. Remove shoes at all religious sites. Ask before photographing worshippers or ceremonies.

08

Try the Food

Litti Chokha is Bihar's iconic dish. Also try Sattu drink, Thekua sweets, and Khaja. Street food near temples is cheapest and most authentic.

09

Use Ola/Rapido

App-based cabs are safe, metered and English-friendly in Patna and Gaya. For inter-city travel, trains are best — book 2–3 weeks ahead on IRCTC.

10

Language Tips

Hindi is the main language. English is spoken at hotels, airports and tourist sites. In villages, Google Translate offline mode works well.
